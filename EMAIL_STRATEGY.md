# Email Strategy Reference

Source: Email Maestro methodology — research-backed best practices  
Version: 2026-06-07

---

## 1. Context-Gathering Workflow (mandatory before any draft)

Do not write a single word of copy until all of the following are confirmed. This is non-negotiable.

**Step A — Mandatory intake (ask and confirm):**
1. Which sector or industry? (business-to-business, business-to-consumer, SaaS, events, media, e-commerce)
2. Primary goal? (lead generation, subscription revenue, event sign-ups, retention, transactional)
3. Target recipient role/seniority and company size (e.g. marketing manager, founder, SME)
4. Is this part of a broader campaign sequence? (timing, previous sends, series position)
5. Brand guidelines and compliance constraints? Any suppression lists or opt-out segments?
6. Any hard facts to cite? (metrics, case studies, uplift percentages)
7. Primary CTA and conversion endpoint (booking, sign-up, download, call, purchase)
8. Desired send window / urgency constraints (real deadlines only — no fabricated urgency)

**Step B — Context enrichment:**
- Identify any previous campaign performance data to inform optimisation (open/CTR/conversion baseline)
- Confirm personalisation data available (name, company, role, behavioural signals)
- Confirm localisation needs (language, terminology, regional references)

**Step C — Selection and mapping:**
- Map campaign goal → behavioural triggers + template type
- Select segmentation slices and personalisation layers to apply

**Step D — Pre-write checklist:**
- Subject line ≤ 50 chars confirmed
- Readability target: Flesch-Kincaid 60–70
- Single primary CTA identified
- Compliance flags checked (consent basis, unsubscribe path, data minimisation)
- All claims verifiable and sourced

Only after Steps A–D are complete, proceed to draft.

## 2. Behavioural Triggers

Apply contextually — never stack more than 2–3 per email.

| Trigger | Description | Example application |
|---|---|---|
| **Endowment / Ownership** | Use "your" language — recipients value what they feel they already own | "Your usage report", "Your account summary" |
| **Present Bias / Authentic Urgency** | Tie deadlines to real events, not fabricated countdowns | "Q3 deadline closes Friday" |
| **Exclusivity / Scarcity** | Real tiered access, limited seats, pilot cohorts — never fake | "Limited to 25 participants" |
| **Social Proof / Credibility** | Industry metrics, relevant case studies — not generic global stats | "How [Company] doubled renewals" |
| **Progression / Commitment** | Next-step framing, progressive rewards, milestone language | "You're one step from full access" |
| **Authority / Expertise** | Verified credentials, data, industry position | "Trusted by 500+ teams" |
| **Reciprocity** | Free resource, audit, or insight relevant to recipient | "Free audit of your current setup" |
| **Loss Aversion** | Frame what they miss vs. what they gain — ethically | "Teams who opted in saw 28% lift" |

## 3. Content Structure

Every email must follow this sequence:

```
Opening Hook
  ↓
Value Proposition (role-specific)
  ↓
Social Proof (specific, verifiable)
  ↓
Single Primary CTA
```

- **Opening hook:** One sentence. No pleasantries. Get to the point.
- **Value prop:** Quantified where possible. Role-specific. No generic benefits.
- **Social proof:** Specific and verifiable. Do not use unsourced global statistics as primary proof.
- **CTA:** One. Primary. Prominent. Repeated once in footer.

---

## 4. Subject Line Rules

- **Maximum 50 characters** (mobile optimisation — non-negotiable)
- **Preview text maximum 75 characters**
- **One psychological hook per subject line** — do not stack
- Avoid spammy punctuation, ALL CAPS, excessive exclamation marks
- Use natural business phrasing — not clichéd idioms
- Subject must accurately reflect email body (no bait-and-switch)

**Priority hierarchy for hook selection:**

```
1. Urgency + Exclusivity   (highest engagement in professional email)
2. Ownership + Value
3. Curiosity + Benefit     (use sparingly — lower trust signal)
```

**Example subject lines by hierarchy:**

- Urgency + Exclusivity: "Last 10 seats: ROI Workshop — closes Friday"
- Ownership + Value: "Your customised usage report — ready now"
- Curiosity + Benefit: "What high-performing teams do differently"

---

## 5. CTA Rules

- **One primary CTA per email.** Optional secondary CTA in footer only.
- CTA copy must use **ownership/progression verbs:**
  - ✅ Reserve, Claim, Secure, Start, Book, Access
  - ❌ Click here, Learn more, Submit, Go
- Format: `Action + Benefit + Low friction`
  - Example: "Reserve your free audit"
  - Example: "Book a 15-min review"
- CTAs must link to a measurable landing page with **UTM tracking**
- CTA promise must be deliverable — no misleading timelines or outcomes
- Visually prominent (button, not text link) and repeated once in footer

## 6. Readability Targets

| Target | Value |
|---|---|
| Flesch-Kincaid reading ease | 60–70 (plain English, 8th–10th grade) |
| Average sentence length | 12–18 words |
| Paragraph length | 1–3 lines max |
| Voice | Active throughout |
| Tone | Professional, direct — not salesy |
| Vocabulary | Simple; avoid jargon unless recipient role demands it |

Use bullets for lists of benefits. Never use bullet points for narrative content.

## 7. Segmentation Dimensions

Segment across all four axes before selecting triggers and template:

| Axis | Options |
|---|---|
| **Sector** | SaaS, professional services, media, e-commerce, events, education |
| **Role / Seniority** | Individual contributor, manager, director, C-suite, founder/SME |
| **Behaviour** | Highly engaged, dormant, first-time opener, repeat clicker |
| **Lifecycle** | Prospect, active user/subscriber, lapsed, long-term client |

Additional dimensions:
- Company size / revenue band (SME, mid-market, enterprise)
- Geography (region, language, timezone)
- Purchase or usage history (plan level, event attendance, renewals)
- Content vertical preference (tech, finance, retail, health)

## 8. Personalisation Layers

Apply in priority order — implement as many as data allows:

1. Recipient name + role (salutation and opening hook)
2. Company name + relevant metric (ownership framing)
3. Sector-specific value proposition (tailored benefit)
4. Relevant social proof (industry-matched case study)
5. Behavioural cue (recent action or inactivity trigger)
6. Offer specificity (seat count, inventory, exclusive access)
7. Dynamic CTA text reflecting next-step for that segment
8. Timing personalisation (real calendar events, product milestones)

Use **progressive profiling** — collect data over time rather than requiring it upfront. Use **dynamic content blocks** where the email service provider supports it.

## 9. A/B Testing Methodology

Always produce 2–3 variants. Never send a single version of a campaign email.

**Test design rules:**
- Test **one variable per test** (subject line OR CTA OR hero benefit copy)
- Prioritise subject line A/B first — highest leverage for deliverability and opens
- Document a hypothesis for each variant before sending

**Split:**
- Randomised, representative split across segment
- Equal distribution: 50/50 (two variants) or 33/33/33 (three variants)
- Holdout control group for major flow changes

**Sample size and duration:**
- Minimum: 1,000 recipients per variant where list size allows
- Open/subject tests: 48–72 hour window
- Conversion tests: 7–14 days (response lag in professional email)
- Use chi-square or z-test for statistical significance (80% power, 5% alpha)

**Reporting:**
- Report: lift %, confidence level, sample sizes, primary metric, secondary metrics (unsub rate, spam complaints)
- Roll winner to remaining list after significance reached
- Apply learnings to next campaign — maintain a running test log

## 10. Compliance Guardrails

**Hard rules — no exceptions:**

- ❌ No fake scarcity or fabricated deadlines
- ❌ No unverifiable claims or made-up metrics
- ❌ No pre-checked opt-in boxes or dark patterns
- ❌ No sensitive personal data referenced in copy
- ✅ Verify lawful basis for processing (consent or legitimate interest) before send
- ✅ Clear, functional unsubscribe link in every commercial email
- ✅ Data minimisation — only use personal details required for the email
- ✅ All promotional terms explicit (pricing, offer conditions, expiry)
- ✅ Secure links — landing pages must handle data appropriately
- ✅ Keep records of all claims and their sources (auditability)

**Applicable frameworks (jurisdiction-dependent — consult your legal team):**
- GDPR (EU/UK): lawful basis, right to erasure, data minimisation, consent records
- CAN-SPAM (US): unsubscribe mechanism, physical address, no deceptive headers
- CASL (Canada): express or implied consent, sender identification
- Other: apply the most restrictive applicable framework if multi-jurisdiction

**General principles that apply everywhere:**
- Respect opt-outs promptly (within 10 business days at most; ideally immediately)
- Never email individuals who have not consented or have no legitimate interest relationship
- All offers must be clear, fair, and not misleading

## 11. Success Metrics

Define targets before sending. Measure against relevant industry benchmarks for your sector.

| Category | Metric | Target indicator |
|---|---|---|
| **Delivery** | Delivery rate | ≥ 98% |
| **Delivery** | Hard bounce rate | < 2% |
| **Engagement** | Open rate | Top quartile vs. sector norm |
| **Engagement** | Unique CTR | Uplift vs. baseline |
| **Engagement** | Click-to-open rate (CTOR) | Improvement vs. baseline (content relevance proxy) |
| **Conversion** | Conversion rate | Campaign-specific KPI (sign-ups, bookings, revenue) |
| **Conversion** | Revenue per email | Track for paid offers |
| **Retention** | Unsubscribe rate | Flag if spikes above baseline — investigate content |
| **Safety** | Spam complaint rate | < 0.1% |
| **List health** | List growth rate | Track monthly |
| **List health** | Reactivation rate | Track for re-engagement campaigns |

**Primary decision metric:** Lift vs. baseline — not absolute numbers in isolation.

## 12. Timing Recommendations

Based on cross-industry professional email research:

| Variable | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Best send days** | Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday |
| **Best send window** | Mid-morning (09:00–11:00 recipient local time) |
| **Avoid** | Monday mornings, Friday afternoons, public holidays |
| **Mobile share** | High — always design mobile-first |
| **Cadence** | Relevance over frequency; unsubscribes spike with irrelevant sends |
| **Re-engagement** | Suppress unengaged contacts after 6 months; run reactivation campaign first |

Always tie urgency to a real calendar event — never to a fabricated countdown.

Use **send-time optimisation (STO)** if your email service provider supports it — it adjusts delivery time per individual based on past open behaviour.
